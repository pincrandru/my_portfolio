Context

The current urban transport system does not always effectively cover all passenger routes, leaving gaps in accessibility and service quality, especially for those seeking more flexible and comfortable solutions than traditional public transport, yet more affordable than a standard taxi. Additionally, taxi drivers often face idle time and inefficient use of their time and resources. The Marshrutka service aims to address these problems by offering an innovative hybrid of traditional taxis and urban public transport. This not only allows city residents to comfortably and quickly reach their destinations, effectively bridging gaps in the transport network, but also provides taxi drivers with stable and predictable income through fixed routes, along with efficient use of their time and fuel. Such a solution improves urban mobility, relieves pressure on existing transport modes, positively impacts the city's economy by increasing economic activity, and ultimately creates a significant business opportunity for Marshrutka, enabling it to scale, optimize operations, and increase revenue through a unique cross-market offering.

Defining the audience

Regular Drivers

For them, taxi driving is their primary source of income. They seek stability, predictability, and optimization of their workflow to maximize profits.

Part-time Drivers

They use taxi driving for additional income in their free time. Schedule flexibility is an important factor for them.

Drivers Seeking Alternatives

Dissatisfied with working conditions in current taxi aggregators (high commissions, suboptimal orders, lack of transparency). They are looking for more favorable and comfortable cooperation terms.

Understanding the context

Where are they physically located?

  • On city roads, in traffic;

  • At taxi stands or waiting for orders;

  • At the starting and ending points of public transport routes;

  • Inside their vehicle (their primary workspace).

What trigger event causes this need?

  • Desire to increase earnings or stabilize income;

  • Idle time;

  • Search for more efficient ways to use fuel and time;

  • Dissatisfaction with current working conditions (e.g., aggregator commissions, unpredictability of orders);

  • Need to end a shift, but desire to maximize the "return" trip.

How much time do they have?

  • Taxi drivers typically work on a flexible schedule, managing their own time. They need a solution that allows them to efficiently utilize both short time intervals and long shifts;

  • Time spent on "empty" runs or waiting for an order is lost time for them.

Audience needs

High-level motivation ‚Üí Maximizing working hours and achieving stable income

They can achieve this through:

  • A stable passenger flow and a predictable route;
  • Route and fuel consumption optimization;
  • Transparency and control over earnings;
  • Flexibility in work.
