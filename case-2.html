Improving ATM user experience

IOS

Task

Develop a solution to enhance the convenience and efficiency of ATM usage.

Defining the goal

ATMs are an integral part of people's financial lives. Although this technology has existed for a long time and is firmly integrated into our lives, the user experience has not fundamentally changed. Improving the ATM user experience will allow clients to reliably, securely, simply, and comfortably perform monetary operations. This will increase trust in the bank and its services, attract new clients, reduce the burden on bank representatives and support staff, expand bank services to underserved audiences, and all of this, accordingly, can lead to increased company revenue.

Defining the audience

The two main and large groups to which the bank extends its services are legal entities and individuals. In this task, we will focus on individuals.

ATMs are used by people of various ages, incomes, social classes, and social groups. However, users can be stratified by their experience and specific characteristics that may influence their user experience.

Youth and middle-aged adults

This group of clients is typically tech-savvy, frequently uses ATMs, and has no difficulty operating them. This is also the largest cohort of bank clients, as they constitute the main part of the economically active population and, accordingly, interact more frequently and extensively with financial services.

Teenagers

Although they are minors and not part of the general workforce cohort, they are increasingly expanding their economic rights and can be bank clients. Parents can issue them debit cards, either as an additional card to their own account or to the teenager's individual account. Modern teenagers have no trouble understanding the technical aspects of an ATM or a mobile application, but they are not as financially literate as adults and require more parental oversight.

Elderly people

Digital products often pose challenges for them, although elderly people are still economically active and require the same access to bank services and products. Improving the ATM user experience can make it appealing to them and also increase loyalty to the bank.

Foreigners

They also need to use ATMs and expect their experience to be considered, enabling them to meet their needs in a language they understand.

People with special health needs

Many health conditions make ATM usage difficult, and improvements in interaction can help clients who need additional features or require the absence of physical limitations.

Understanding the context and audience needs

ATMs are placed in locations that allow quick access at the client's convenience, usually near places where people conduct financial transactions. Therefore, these are typically crowded areas, such as city centers, high-concentration areas in districts (shops and shopping centers), and bank branches.

If the ATM is located outdoors, it must be used in various weather conditions ‚Äî it may be snowing, raining, or the sun may be shining and glinting off the screen. Also, queues often form around ATMs, which can make people nervous and put pressure on the person currently using the machine.

Clients expect ATMs to be available 24/7, especially if they are outdoors, and in some cases, inside buildings and bank branches after closing hours. Users also count on round-the-clock support if something goes wrong.

Furthermore, users expect the ATM to be maintained ‚Äî it should be clean, buttons should work, cash should be consistently supplied in various denominations, foreign currency should be available, all functions should be accessible, and the machine should generally be operational.

Contactless payment has become widespread and common, so ATMs should support it. Along with this, popular payment systems should be supported, as well as a limited range of actions that can be performed without needing to remember a PIN code.

An important aspect remains security and ensuring the confidentiality of the actions a client performs at the ATM.

Attention should also be paid to the emotional aspect of using an ATM. The entire user experience should be clear, simple, comfortable, stress-free, prevent errors, or report them correctly.

Separately, accessibility elements should be considered ‚Äì Braille, audio assistant, absence of physical obstacles, convenient placement of the machine.

Main functions offered by an ATM:

  • Cash deposit and withdrawal;

  • Account balance inquiry;

  • Statement printing;

  • Bill payments;

  • Money transfers to other accounts;

  • Foreign currency withdrawal;

  • Loan payments and deposit replenishment;

  • Card operations (activation, blocking, PIN change, etc).

Among all the listed functions, cash operations ‚Äî both withdrawal and deposit ‚Äî and in some cases, statement printing, are exclusive to ATMs. This is an important factor to consider when developing the design solution.

Ideation

The following ideas emerged from the brainstorming process:

  • Interface redesign;
  • Simplified mode;
  • Contextual adaptation;
  • Personalized ATM;
  • Physical hardware redesign.

Personalized ATM

Users will be able to set up a list of 'favorite' and most frequent operations via mobile banking, which will be quickly accessible on the ATM screen after contactless identification, minimizing steps and simplifying interaction.

This solution will allow for the continuation of the concept of mobile application integration with the ATM.

Prioritization and idea selection

Here's an overview of how the design approach caters to different user segments:

Youth and middle-aged adults

This group is tech-savvy, so this feature can help them save a valuable resource ‚Äî time. Although they perform most operations via mobile or internet banking, they sometimes need to conduct cash transactions. This feature will allow them to use the ATM even faster.

Teenagers

They have no trouble understanding how various technologies work, but a key specificity of this user group is their insufficient financial literacy and parental control over their financial activities. Fast functions will help teenagers lower the barrier to monetary and banking operations, learn to perform them earlier, avoid getting lost in unnecessary functions, and give parents a bit more control over their children's money.

Elderly people

We will prioritize this group separately in this solution. The ability to set up a few of the most important functions for them via a mobile application with the help of relatives, bank employees, or through "smart prompts" in mobile banking will reduce their fear of the ATM.

People with special health needs

The diversity of health conditions complicates coverage assessment. Our feature, by reducing on-screen elements and making them larger and clearer, along with audio assistant integration, will significantly enhance user convenience and accessibility. While physical ATM accessibility (height, absence of obstacles) remains crucial, simplifying the interface minimizes movements, offering a valuable additional benefit.

Foreigners and tourists

Our solution will have less coverage for this group. They will likely find a standard multilingual interface more useful.

This solution directly addresses key customer needs:

  • It speeds up the usage process;
  • Fewer steps and an uncluttered interface simplify usage;
  • It reduces stress and tension;
  • It increases ATM accessibility;
  • It ensures security through modern identification methods.

This solution will only indirectly affect the company's revenue:

  • Increased customer loyalty;
  • Attraction of new customers;
  • Reduced operational costs due to fewer support and bank staff inquiries;
  • Innovative and inclusive solutions will positively impact the company's brand.

Design solution

Access to ATM personalization is available from the main screen, within the quick access functions area.

The user can choose from a wide list of operations or use system suggestions, which are generated based on their frequent actions. Operations can also be deleted individually or cleared entirely.

Operations can be renamed and flexibly customized.

The user scans a QR code if contactless identification is unavailable, or uses NFC on their phone or card. After this, the user journey continues on the ATM interface.

On the ATM's splash screen, a QR code can be found and used for identification.

After this, the user sees a personalized functions screen with the option to switch to the full ATM interface mode.

After selecting an operation, the user must go through authorization‚Ä¶

  3. Increased feature usage frequency:

    4. The number of unique users regularly utilizing the operation personalization feature in the mobile application and/or connecting to the ATM via QR/NFC.

    High level of engagement with the new functionality.

  4. Growth in customer satisfaction score:

    5. Measurement of customers' willingness to recommend the bank and its services (specifically, the improved ATM user experience) to others.

    6. Decreased number of client inquiries related to difficulties using the ATM or the need for assistance from bank employees.
